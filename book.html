Note, Questions, Uncertainties

Disclaimer: I stumbled upon Di viole e liquirizia years ago. After an Alitalia in-flight magazine called it "An Italian Sideways," I bought a copy as motivation to learn Italian, but mostly forgot about it. In 2014, after an amazing vacation in Piedmont, I realized the novel was set there, and that I really wanted to read it. As far as I can tell, it's never been published in English, so I spent most of 2015 slowly translating it. The result, though far from perfect, was better than I expected. I posted it online so friends could see it, and so I could correct it as my Italian improved.

I'm not a writer, and let's say I'm not at an optimal age for language acquisition, so it's full of mistranslations, both known and inadvertent, educated guesses, wild guesses, typos, grammatical errors, misspellings, and other clunkers.

The novel's protagonist is Daniel, a Parisian sommelier who comes to Piedmont to give a series of wine-tasting classes at a wine-bar in Alba. Daniel is running away from problems in Paris, but becomes involved with the owner of the wine-bar, Amalia, who has her own problems. Despite the clunkers, if you're interested in wine, and especially the wine (and food) of the Piedmont region, you may enjoy it. I certainly did.
